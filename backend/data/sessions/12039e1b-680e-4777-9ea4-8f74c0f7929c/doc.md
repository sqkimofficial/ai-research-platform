## Introduction

Augmented Reality (AR) is a transformative technology that blends the digital and physical worlds, enhancing our perception of reality by overlaying digital information onto the real environment. Unlike Virtual Reality (VR), which immerses users in a fully virtual space, AR enriches the user's real-world experience by integrating digital elements, such as images, sounds, and other sensory stimuli. The evolution of AR can be traced back to various technological advancements, which have paved the way for its current applications in gaming, education, healthcare, and numerous other fields.

As AR continues to advance, it is reshaping how we interact with our surroundings and the information we consume, making it a pivotal area of study in the technology landscape.

## Background

The concept of augmented reality has its roots in the late 20th century but has gained significant traction in recent years due to advancements in technology and hardware capabilities. The journey of AR began with simple experiments and has evolved into complex systems used in various industries today. This section outlines the historical milestones that have shaped augmented reality into what it is now.

| Year | Contributor | Contribution Description |
|------|-------------|-------------------------|
| 1968 | Ivan Sutherland | Developed the first head-mounted display system, known as the Sword of Damocles, which laid the groundwork for AR technology. |
| 1990 | Tom Caudell | Coined the term "augmented reality" while working at Boeing to describe how digital information could enhance the assembly process. |
| 1992 | Louis Rosenberg | Created the Virtual Fixtures system, one of the first fully functional AR systems used in a real-world environment. |
| 1999 | Bruce Thomas | Developed ARQuake, the first AR game that allowed players to navigate real-world environments while interacting with virtual elements. |
| 2000 | Hirokazu Kato | Released ARToolKit, a widely used software library for developing AR applications, which significantly lowered the barrier for developers. |

Recent innovations in augmented reality (AR) have been marked by significant advancements in hardware and software, enabling a wider array of applications across various industries. Companies like Apple and Google are investing heavily in AR technology, with Apple rumored to be developing AR glasses that could revolutionize personal technology interactions. Furthermore, the integration of AR in e-commerce, education, and training has gained traction, allowing for immersive experiences that enhance user engagement and learning outcomes. As AR continues to evolve, its potential to transform everyday activities and business operations becomes more apparent, signaling a promising future for this technology.

Oculus VR has made significant strides in the realm of augmented reality and virtual reality since its inception. Originally known for its development of the Oculus Rift, a pioneering virtual reality headset, the company has expanded its focus to include mixed reality experiences. With the release of products like the Oculus Quest and Meta Quest Pro, Oculus has provided users with standalone devices that blend virtual and augmented reality, allowing for immersive interactions in both digital and physical spaces. Their advancements in tracking technology, user interface design, and accessibility have set new standards in the industry, fostering a growing interest in augmented reality applications across gaming, education, and professional training sectors.
