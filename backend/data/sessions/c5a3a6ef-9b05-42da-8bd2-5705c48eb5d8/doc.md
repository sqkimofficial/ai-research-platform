## Introduction

Augmented Reality (AR) is a transformative technology that blends digital information with the real world, creating an interactive experience for users. By overlaying computer-generated images, sounds, and other sensory enhancements onto the physical environment, AR enhances the user's perception and understanding of their surroundings. Emerging from the convergence of computer vision, graphics, and simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM), AR has found applications across various fields, including gaming, education, healthcare, and industry. This document explores the historical evolution of AR, its foundational technologies, and significant milestones that have shaped its development.

## Background

The concept of augmented reality has evolved over several decades, with its roots tracing back to earlier technological innovations. The journey of AR is marked by pioneering efforts in computer graphics, virtual reality, and user interface design. Notably, developments in hardware and software have played crucial roles in making AR accessible and applicable in real-world scenarios. As AR continues to advance, understanding its historical context provides insight into its current applications and future potential.

Recent advancements in augmented reality have been marked by significant innovations and increased investment from major technology companies. In 2021, Meta (formerly Facebook) announced its vision for the metaverse, emphasizing the integration of AR and VR technologies to create immersive virtual environments. Additionally, the release of the Meta Quest Pro in 2022 showcased high-end features that support AR applications, further pushing the boundaries of what is possible in augmented reality. Moreover, companies like Niantic continue to enhance AR experiences with developments in location-based gaming, while advancements in AR glasses and wearable technologies promise to make AR more accessible and practical for everyday use.

Oculus VR has played a pivotal role in the evolution of augmented reality and virtual reality technologies. Founded in 2012 and later acquired by Facebook in 2014, Oculus has focused on developing high-quality, consumer-grade virtual reality headsets that have influenced the AR landscape. The Oculus Quest series, particularly the Quest 2, has integrated AR capabilities, allowing users to interact with their environment in immersive ways. Through advancements in hardware and software, Oculus has made virtual reality more accessible, paving the way for innovative AR applications and experiences.
