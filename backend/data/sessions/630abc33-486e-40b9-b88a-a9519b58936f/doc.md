## Introduction

Augmented Reality (AR) has emerged as a transformative technology that enhances our perception of reality by overlaying digital information onto the physical world. This innovative interface between the real and virtual environments has found applications across various fields, including education, healthcare, gaming, and manufacturing. Understanding the evolution of augmented reality is crucial to appreciating its current applications and future potential. This research document aims to explore the historical milestones that have shaped augmented reality, starting from its inception to the present day.

In recent years, AR has gained significant attention due to advancements in technology and innovative applications. Companies like Microsoft, Apple, and Snap Inc. have made headlines with their developments in AR glasses, mobile applications, and immersive experiences that integrate digital elements into everyday life. Additionally, the COVID-19 pandemic accelerated the adoption of AR for remote collaboration, virtual meetings, and enhanced customer experiences in retail. As AR continues to evolve, it is poised to redefine how we interact with the world around us, making it a focal point in discussions about the future of technology.

## Background

The concept of augmented reality can be traced back to the early 20th century, when the foundations of the technology began to take shape through various inventions and ideas. Over the decades, advancements in computing power, graphics, and user interface design have propelled the development of AR technologies. The following table summarizes key historical milestones in the progression of augmented reality, highlighting significant contributions and breakthroughs in the field.

| Year | Contributor               | Contribution Description                                          |
|------|---------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1968 | Ivan Sutherland            | Developed the first head-mounted display system, "The Sword of Damocles." |
| 1990 | Tom Caudell               | Coined the term "augmented reality" while working at Boeing.   |
| 1992 | Louis Rosenberg           | Created the Virtual Fixtures system, an early AR application for industrial use. |
| 1999 | Hirokazu Kato            | Developed ARToolKit, an open-source software library for AR applications. |
| 2000 | Paul Milgram and Fumio Kishino | Introduced a taxonomy for AR systems based on their interaction with the real world. |
| 2013 | Google                   | Released Google Glass, a pioneering hands-free AR device.       |
| 2016 | Niantic                  | Launched Pokémon GO, a mobile AR game that popularized AR for mainstream audiences. |
