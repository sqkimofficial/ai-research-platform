## Introduction

Augmented reality (AR) has emerged as a transformative technology that enhances the real world by overlaying digital information and media onto physical environments. This immersive experience has gained significant traction across various fields, including gaming, education, healthcare, and training. Understanding the evolution of AR is crucial to grasp its current applications and future potential.

This document aims to explore the history of augmented reality, tracing its development from early conceptualizations to modern implementations that have reshaped user interactions with digital content.

## Background

The roots of augmented reality can be traced back to the mid-20th century, with significant milestones that paved the way for contemporary AR technologies. This section outlines the key developments and figures in the history of AR.

| Year | Innovator/Group | Contribution |
|------|-----------------|--------------|
| 1968 | Ivan Sutherland   | Developed the first head-mounted display system, known as the "Sword of Damocles." |
| 1990 | Louis Rosenberg   | Created the first fully functional AR system, called "Virtual Fixtures," for the U.S. Air Force. |
| 1992 | Tom Caudell       | Coined the term "augmented reality" while working at Boeing to describe a digital display for assembly workers. |
| 1999 | Hirokazu Kato     | Developed ARToolKit, a pioneering open-source software library for building AR applications. |
| 2009 | Google           | Introduced Google Goggles, an image recognition application that allowed users to search for information using images. |
| 2016 | Pokémon Company   | Launched Pokémon GO, a widely popular mobile AR game that brought AR into mainstream awareness. |

These milestones demonstrate the gradual advancement of augmented reality technologies and their increasing integration into everyday life. As we delve deeper into the subject, we will examine how these innovations have influenced various sectors and set the stage for future developments.
