## Introduction

Augmented Reality (AR) is a transformative technology that overlays digital information onto the physical world, enhancing the user's perception and interaction with their real environment. The integration of AR into various sectors, including education, healthcare, and entertainment, has revolutionized how we interact with technology. Understanding the historical context of AR provides insight into its evolution and the technological advancements that have facilitated its development.

## Background

The concept of augmented reality dates back several decades, with its roots in the fields of computer graphics and human-computer interaction. Early experiments laid the groundwork for what would become a significant technological innovation. This section explores the historical milestones in augmented reality, highlighting key figures and their contributions.

| Year | Contributor         | Contribution Description                          |
|------|---------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| 1968 | Ivan Sutherland      | Developed the first head-mounted display system, known as the "Sword of Damocles." |
| 1990 | Tom Caudell          | Coined the term "augmented reality" while working at Boeing to describe digital displays in manufacturing. |
| 1992 | Louis Rosenberg      | Created the Virtual Fixtures system, one of the first functional AR systems for use in manufacturing. |
| 1999 | Hirokazu Kato       | Developed ARToolKit, an open-source software that enabled the creation of AR applications. |
| 2009 | Google              | Introduced Google Goggles, an image recognition application that provided information based on visual input. |
| 2013 | Niantic Labs        | Released Ingress, a mobile game that popularized location-based AR interactions. |
| 2016 | Pokémon Company     | Launched Pokémon GO, a mobile game that brought AR to mainstream audiences, redefining mobile gaming experiences. |
| 2020 | Microsoft           | Released the HoloLens 2, advancing AR technology for enterprise applications. |
| 2021 | Facebook (Meta)     | Rebranded to Meta, emphasizing the development of the metaverse and AR technologies to enhance social interactions.
| 2022 | Oculus (Meta)       | Launched the Meta Quest Pro, a high-end VR headset with advanced AR capabilities to facilitate immersive experiences.
