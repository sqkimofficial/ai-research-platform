Introduction

Augmented Reality (AR) is an innovative technology that enhances the real world by overlaying digital information onto it. As a fusion of the digital and physical realms, AR has transformed various industries, including education, healthcare, entertainment, and retail. The evolution of AR is closely tied to advancements in computer vision, graphics processing, and mobile technology, which have collectively enabled immersive experiences that were once considered the realm of science fiction. Understanding the history of AR is crucial to appreciating its current applications and potential future developments.

The origins of augmented reality can be traced back to the 1960s, when computer scientists began experimenting with ways to integrate digital content with real-world environments. Over the decades, significant milestones have marked the evolution of AR technology. The following table summarizes key historical developments in augmented reality, highlighting the contributors and their achievements.

Furthermore, the emergence of AR in social media platforms and retail environments is creating more interactive and engaging experiences for users. As the technology continues to evolve, it holds the promise of transforming how we interact with the world around us.

The following table summarizes key historical developments in augmented reality, highlighting the contributors and their achievements.

| Year | Contributor         | Contribution                                      |
|------|---------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| 1968 | Ivan Sutherland      | Developed the first head-mounted display (HMD) known as the Sword of Damocles. |
| 1990 | Louis Rosenberg      | Created the Virtual Fixtures system, an early AR setup for medical applications. |
| 1992 | Tom Caudell         | Coined the term "augmented reality" during his work at Boeing.  |
| 1999 | Hirokazu Kato       | Developed ARToolKit, a pioneering open-source AR software library. |
| 2000 | Paul Milgram        | Published foundational research on the taxonomy of AR systems. |
| 2013 | Google              | Launched Google Glass, a wearable AR device that popularized the technology. |
| 2016 | Niantic             | Released Pokémon GO, a mobile game that brought AR to the mainstream audience. |
| 2020 | Facebook            | Announced the Spark AR platform, enabling users to create and share AR effects on social media. |
| 2021 | Microsoft           | Launched Mesh, a collaborative platform leveraging AR for remote teamwork and communication. |
| 2022 | Meta                | Expanded Oculus platform to include augmented reality features and applications. |
| 2023 | Snap Inc.           | Introduced new AR capabilities in Snapchat, enhancing user interaction and engagement. |
