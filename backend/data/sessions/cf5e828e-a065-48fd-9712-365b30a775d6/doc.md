## Introduction

Augmented reality (AR) has emerged as one of the most transformative technologies in recent years, blending digital information with the physical world to enhance user experiences. This innovative technology has found applications across various sectors, including education, healthcare, entertainment, and retail. Its development has been driven by advancements in computer vision, display technologies, and mobile computing. As AR continues to evolve, understanding its historical context becomes essential to appreciate its current capabilities and future potential.

## Background

The history of augmented reality can be traced back to the early experiments in computer graphics and virtual environments. The foundational work laid by researchers in the fields of computer science and engineering paved the way for the development of AR applications.

### Historical Progress Table

| Year | Contributor(s)          | Contribution Description                                      |
|------|-------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1968 | Ivan Sutherland          | Developed the first head-mounted display system, known as the "Sword of Damocles." |
| 1990 | Tom Caudell             | Coined the term "augmented reality" while working at Boeing to assist in assembly line tasks. |
| 1992 | Louis Rosenberg         | Created the Virtual Fixtures system, which blended virtual objects with real environments for robotic surgery. |
| 1999 | Hirokazu Kato           | Developed ARToolKit, an open-source software library for creating AR applications. |
| 2000 | The MIT Media Lab       | Released the SixthSense prototype, a wearable device that projected information onto surfaces. |
| 2013 | Google                  | Introduced Google Glass, a wearable AR device that displayed information in a hands-free format. |
| 2016 | Niantic                 | Launched Pokémon GO, a mobile AR game that popularized AR technology among the general public. |
| 2020 | Microsoft               | Released the HoloLens 2, an advanced mixed-reality headset for enterprise applications. |
