## Introduction

Augmented Reality (AR) is a transformative technology that enhances the real world by overlaying digital information onto physical environments. By merging the virtual and real worlds, AR opens up new avenues for interaction, learning, and entertainment. Its applications span diverse fields, including education, healthcare, gaming, and architecture, making it a pivotal area of research and development in contemporary technology.

The evolution of AR can be traced back to the mid-20th century, with early experiments laying the groundwork for the sophisticated systems we see today. This research document aims to explore the historical progression of augmented reality, highlighting significant contributions, technological advancements, and the implications of AR in modern society.
