## Introduction

Augmented reality (AR) has emerged as a transformative technology that overlays digital information onto the real world, enhancing the user's perception and interaction with their environment. From its inception to its current applications in various fields such as gaming, education, and healthcare, AR has evolved significantly. This research document explores the historical development of augmented reality, highlighting key milestones and contributions that have shaped this innovative technology.

In recent news, the integration of augmented reality in various sectors has gained momentum, particularly in retail and remote collaboration. Companies are leveraging AR to create immersive shopping experiences, allowing customers to visualize products in their own environment before making purchases. This trend reflects the growing importance of AR in enhancing consumer engagement and providing innovative solutions in today's digital landscape.

## Background

The concept of augmented reality can be traced back several decades, with roots in both science fiction and early technological advancements. The journey of AR is marked by pivotal moments that have contributed to its evolution into a widely used tool today. This section outlines the significant events and developments in the history of augmented reality, emphasizing the contributions of notable individuals and organizations.

| Year | Contributor | Contribution |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| 1968 | Ivan Sutherland | Created the first head-mounted display system, often regarded as the first AR system. |
| 1990 | Tom Caudell | Coined the term "augmented reality" while working at Boeing to describe a digital display system for assembly workers. |
| 1992 | Louis Rosenberg | Developed the Virtual Fixtures system, an early AR system for remote manipulation of objects. |
| 1999 | Hirokazu Kato | Released ARToolKit, an open-source software for developing AR applications using computer vision. |
| 2009 | Google | Introduced Google Maps with AR features, allowing users to navigate using real-time overlays. |
| 2016 | Pokémon Company | Launched Pokémon GO, a mobile game that popularized AR technology among the general public. |
| 2020 | Apple | Released ARKit, enabling developers to create AR experiences on iOS devices, further integrating AR into everyday applications.
